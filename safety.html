Safety guidelines

Meeting strangers is the whole point — here's how to do it wisely.
The golden rule: first meetings always happen in public places, during reasonable hours, with your own way home.

Before you go

  • Tell a friend where you're going, with whom, and when you expect to be back.
  • Check the meeting point — pick busy, public spots (cafés, parks, stations), never private homes for a first meet.
  • Chat in the hangout chat first — a short exchange tells you a lot about the vibe.

While you're there

  • Keep your phone charged and with you.
  • Arrange your own transport — don't depend on someone you just met to get home.
  • Watch your drink and your belongings, as you would anywhere.
  • Trust your gut. If something feels off, leave. You owe nobody an explanation.

If something goes wrong

  • In an emergency, call your local emergency number first.
  • Report bad behavior to us via the contact form — include the hangout title and the person's name. We take reports seriously and remove people who make others unsafe.

Hosting a hangout?

  • Pick an easy-to-find public meeting point and be on time.
  • Say hi to everyone — you set the tone.
  • If someone makes the group uncomfortable, you can ask them to leave and report them to us.
